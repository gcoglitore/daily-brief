I.Middle East & CENTCOM Theater ▼
IRAN / STRAIT OF HORMUZ CRITICAL
1.(S) President Trump extended the U.S.-Iran ceasefire on April 22 to allow continued negotiations; however, Iran's navy on April 22 seized two container ships in the Strait of Hormuz, and three additional cargo vessels were attacked in the waterway. Iranian FM Araghchi declared that "blockading Iranian ports is an act of war." The U.S. naval blockade of Iranian ports remains in effect.
2.(S) On April 23, President Trump ordered the U.S. Navy to destroy any Iranian boats laying mines in the strait. Iran had previously lost track of mines it planted, making full reopening of the strait impossible. U.S. Navy destroyers entered the strait on April 11 for the first time since the war began, conducting mine clearance operations.
3.(S) The Strait of Hormuz — 34km wide at its narrowest point — facilitates approximately 20 million barrels of oil per day, representing ~20% of global seaborne oil trade. China receives approximately one-third of its oil via this route. Sustained closure carries severe global economic consequences.
4.(S) Operation Epic Fury devastated Iran's missile production facilities, stockpiles, and launch capabilities. Iran's Lavan Island oil refinery and Sirri Island crude export facilities sustained significant damage post-ceasefire. Iran restored internet access on April 17 after a 47-day blackout, enabling Iran-aligned hacktivist groups to resume global operations.
// KEY JUDGMENT — HIGH CONFIDENCE
The ceasefire remains fragile. Iran's seizure of commercial vessels and continued mining of the Strait represents active ceasefire violations. A military confrontation between U.S. and Iranian naval forces in the Strait is assessed as a near-term risk without diplomatic resolution. Iran's cyber capabilities are now being reconstituted following internet restoration.
ISRAEL / LEBANON / HEZBOLLAH ELEVATED
5.(S) A ceasefire in Lebanon took effect April 17. Despite the truce, Israeli troops remain present and are actively fighting Hezbollah militants in Lebanon's south. Israel continues consolidation of a buffer zone in southern Lebanon. Hezbollah rocket fire on northern Israel has continued intermittently.
6.(S) The U.S.-Iran ceasefire agreement included a provision requiring Iran to re-open the Strait of Hormuz; however, following U.S. continuation of the Iranian port blockade, Iran re-imposed restrictions, indicating Iran views Lebanon ceasefire linkage as essential to any comprehensive settlement.
U.S. CENTCOM NAVAL POSTURE CRITICAL
7.(S) Three U.S. carrier strike groups are now operating in or en route to the CENTCOM AOR — an unprecedented concentration of naval power not seen since Operation Iraqi Freedom in 2003. The USS Abraham Lincoln (CVN-72) is enforcing the Iranian port blockade in the northern Arabian Sea. The USS Gerald R. Ford (CVN-78) is operating in the Red Sea. The USS George H.W. Bush (CVN-77), with escorts DDG-75 Donald Cook, DDG-87 Mason, and DDG-71 Ross, is transiting around southern Africa and is en route to reinforce the theater.
II.Indo-Pacific & INDOPACOM Theater ▼
TAIWAN STRAIT / PLA OPERATIONS HIGH
1.(S) The Chinese carrier Liaoning conducted its first Taiwan Strait transit since December on April 20, 2026 — a deliberate demonstration of PLA naval capability timed to follow Japan's JS Ikazuchi destroyer transit of the strait. China conducted East China Sea joint combat readiness patrols on April 18 and lodged a strong diplomatic protest with Tokyo.
2.(S) The PLA conducted simultaneous cross-theater exercises in the Philippine Sea and South China Sea. This multi-theater operational pattern represents an elevated readiness posture consistent with PLA preparations for a Taiwan contingency. The PRC has sustained an elevated operational tempo in the strait since Taiwan President William Lai's inauguration.
3.(S) The U.S., Japan, and Philippines conducted joint live-fire exercises facing the Taiwan Strait, signaling combined deterrence. USS George Washington (CVN-73), homeported at Yokosuka, Japan, remains the primary FDNF carrier asset in the INDOPACOM theater.

Risk of miscalculation in the strait is elevated given concurrent CENTCOM commitments limiting available U.S. naval assets for INDOPACOM.

Russia reported 900 soldiers killed in the past 24-hour period per Ukrainian General Staff.

The Kremlin continues to set conditions for possible future aggression against Baltic states.
3.(S) Russia launched more drone strikes against Ukrainian targets in March 2026 than any other month of the war; however, Ukrainian forces intercepted 92% of them. Ukraine has intensified its drone and sea-drone campaign against Russian oil infrastructure at Ust-Luga, Primorsk, and other hubs, damaging approximately 20% of Russia's refining capacity since early 2024.

NATO's DEFCON 3 posture reflects this assessment. The Kremlin's Baltic conditioning activities represent strategic preparation, not mere signaling.
